About the Foundation

The Dannonmbi Care Foundation is the nonprofit arm of the Dannon Group, committed to empowering individuals and families affected by crises and natural disasters. Founded to bridge the gap between immediate relief and long-term recovery, the Foundation focuses on trauma recovery, resilience-building, and community development, particularly in underserved communities across Nigeria.

Since its establishment, the foundation has delivered impactful programmes including Annual Grants for large-scale initiatives and grassroots projects, as well as Rapid Response Grants, activated within 48 hours of crises to provide critical aid. These programmes have directly benefited communities in regions like Kaduna and Maiduguri, offering culturally sensitive and community-driven intervention that have helped thousands recover, rebuild, and thrive.

By integrating funding with technical support, collaborating with local partners, and maintain a commitment to transparency, the Dannonmbi Care Foundation ensures sustainable and measurable outcomes. Looking ahead, we aspire to expand our reach by establishing trauma recovery centers, enhancing data-driven solutions, and fostering stronger partnerships. We invite you to join us - whether through donations, volunteering, or collaborations – as we build resilient communities across Nigeria.

Strengthening crises-affected individuals and families to heal, rebuild, and thrive through care, resources, and resilience-focused support.

Building resilient communities where every individual and family can heal, rebuild, and grow after crises.

Dannonmbi Care Foundation aims to:

  • Deliver trauma-informed care and essential resources to help individuals and families recover from crises and rebuild their lives.
  • Provide resilience-building programmes that empower communities toward self-sufficiency, stability, and preparedness for future challenges.
  • Advocate for mental health awareness, disaster readiness, and equal access to recovery resources for underserved and vulnerable populations.

Dannonmbi Care Foundation Core Values:

  • Compassion: Centering care and empathy in everything we do.
  • Integrity: Sustaining transparency, trust, and accountability.
  • Empowerment: Providing tools and resources to rebuild after crises.

Dannonmbi Care Foundation works to strengthen the capacity of families and individuals affected by crises and natural disasters, helping them recover from trauma and rebuild their lives. Through a network of local and international partners, including Non-Governmental Organisations, Community-Based Organisations, Government agencies, Faith-Based Organisations, and Corporate Partners, we ensure that the right support is provided where it can have the greatest impact. Our approach is built on collaboration with diverse stakeholders, from academic institutions and mental health networks to youth-led organisations and volunteer groups. We work closely with local community leaders and traditional rulers to ensure our programmes are culturally relevant, effective, and aligned with the needs of the communities we serve. The Foundation also collaborates with multilateral agencies, like the United Nations and other global aid organisations, boosting our efforts and mobilizing broader support. These partnerships enhance our capacity to respond effectively and ensure the sustainability of our programmes.

    At Dannonmbi Care Foundation, grant-making is more than funding—it’s a pathway to empowering communities, fostering resilience, and driving sustainable recovery. Aligned with our mission to create thriving communities, our approach emphasizes transparency, accountability, collaboration, and adaptability while addressing both urgent and long-term needs across trauma recovery, resilience-building, and community development.

    Our grant-making process is categorized into two key areas: Annual Grants and Rapid Response Grants.

  • Annual Grants:

    • Annual Grants form the backbone of our funding efforts, enabling impactful, sustainable projects across Nigeria. Each year, we publish a Call for Concept Notes, inviting eligible organisations to submit proposals. Our process is rigorous, transparent, and inclusive, involving eligibility screening, proposal assessment, and board-level reviews. Feedback is provided to promote trust, learning, and continuous improvement. Annual Grants are further divided into Main Grants and Community Support Funds.

  • Main Grants:

    • These large-scale grants are available to Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs) and Community-Based Organisations (CBOs) nationwide. They focus on initiatives such as trauma recovery, mental health support, and resilience-building programmes. Applications are evaluated for alignment with our mission, feasibility, and potential for sustainable impact. A structured timeline ensures decisions are finalized within six months of submission, promoting clarity and efficiency.

  • Community Support Funds:

    • Tailored for grassroots organisations, particularly in Kaduna and Maiduguri—our priority states—these smaller grants address culturally relevant, locally driven projects. Beyond financial support, we provide technical and institutional assistance to enhance capacity and foster community engagement. These grants aim to build long-term self-sufficiency while addressing immediate needs.

  • Rapid Response Grants:

    • During crises, our Rapid Response Grants enable swift humanitarian relief, activated within 48 hours of an emergency. These grants address urgent needs such as mental health care, emergency supplies, and temporary shelter while laying the groundwork for long-term recovery.

    We collaborate closely with local leaders, NGOs, and multilateral organisations to ensure effective and accountable delivery. Guided by data analysis and risk assessments, our responses are targeted, efficient, and adaptable. Post-crisis reflections allow us to assess strengths, address weaknesses, and improve future protocols. Interventions are implemented within 30 days, with continuous evaluations informing future actions.

    At every stage, we prioritise transparency, accountability, and learning. Feedback mechanisms, data reviews, and regular assessments refine our strategies, ensuring every project achieves measurable, lasting results. By embedding these principles into all our grant schemes, we empower communities to heal, rebuild, and thrive for generations to come.
